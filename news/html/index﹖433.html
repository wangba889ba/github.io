mountaineering

A category of sports. Athletes climb various peaks or ridges in different terrains with bare hands or specialized equipment.
The practice of human life and production labor is the foundation and origin of sports, and mountaineering is also the same: the emergence and development of mountaineering technology first comes from the practice of people's life and production labor. Many parts of the world have mountains, including the Himalayas that rise into the sky and small mountains with an altitude of only a few hundred meters, such as Xiangshan in Beijing and Qianfo Mountain in Jinan. Almost all countries on Earth have mountains. As the saying goes, "relying on mountains to eat mountains, relying on water to eat water." People who have lived on the mountains or in areas near them for a long time obtain various means of livelihood and production by going up the mountains to cut firewood, hunting, logging, and excavating wild fruits, vegetables, medicinal herbs, and mineral resources. In ancient times, when floods flooded, people went up the mountains to avoid them. After the emergence of tribes, ethnic groups, and countries in human society, people often go up the mountains to avoid invading enemies or rely on the mountains to strike enemies. After the formation of commodity exchanges, people catch up with horses and cross mountains to exchange goods, culture, and art with the Xibu tribe. The entire human life is closely related to mountains, and mountaineering has continuously developed as a result. In China, there are still many traditional customs of mountaineering among the people, such as using the Double Ninth Festival on September 9th to climb high Many literati and literati also love traveling for fitness and tourism activities: climbing mountains to fully enjoy the magnificent scenery of "meeting the top of the mountain and seeing the small mountains". In order to reach the summit, people fully utilize their intelligence and wisdom. Countless years ago, someone used specialized equipment such as mountain picks and ropes, and mastered knowledge related to snow, rolling stones, ice avalanches, and hypoxia in high mountains. After the formation of specialized mountaineering equipment, mountaineering gradually separated from travel activities and became an independent sports event. The use of mountaineering as a specialized sport began in the late 18th century.
